How our lead-routing engine works

This is a live, interactive demo of how an incoming mortgage lead gets matched to one of our licensed loan officers — or sent to the Prequal Team. Generate a lead and the engine decides where it goes, and tells you exactly why. The officers, NMLS numbers, and state licensing shown here are real.

The rules (checked in this exact order)

  1. Disqualifiers go to Prequal. If a lead's credit is under 580 or assets are under $5,000, it skips the officers entirely and goes to the Prequal Team.
  2. Match the license. We keep only the officers licensed in the lead's state. Each LO covers specific states (shown under their name).
  3. Skip anyone paused. A paused officer is removed from consideration for new leads.
  4. Primary officers first. Some officers are fallback / overflow (e.g. Bryan, who's licensed almost everywhere). A fallback officer only receives a lead when no primary officer is available in that state — so they don't vacuum up volume that should go to the local LOs. They still fully own any state no primary covers.
  5. Round robin the rest. When several officers qualify in the same tier (e.g. GA is covered by two primaries), leads alternate evenly so nobody gets overloaded.
  6. No match? Prequal. If no licensed, available officer remains, the lead falls back to the Prequal Team.

How to drive the demo

  • Generate Random Lead — fires a random lead through the engine. Watch the card land and read the log line.
  • Scenario presets — each button forces one specific path: a sub-580 lead, a low-assets lead, a GA lead (click it a few times to watch round robin cycle through the GA-licensed officers), and a New York lead — a state none of these officers cover — that falls back to Prequal.
  • Pause / Resume — pause a whole officer and the next leads route around them.
  • Pause a single state — click any state chip under an officer to pause just that state (it turns red and strikes through). They keep taking leads everywhere else. Click again to resume.
  • Make fallback / Make primary — flip any officer between tiers. Watch a fallback officer's chips: solid = states they actively own, outlined = states they only back up.
  • Already-assigned cards stay put — pausing only affects new leads.
  • Routing Log (right side) — one plain-English line per lead explaining why it routed where it did.
  • Reset Demo — clears everything to start fresh.

Routing logic (applied in this exact order)

  1. Disqualifiers → Prequal Team if credit < 580 OR assets < $5,000.
  2. State licensing — keep only officers licensed in the lead's state.
  3. Drop paused officers from the eligible pool — whether paused entirely or just for the lead's state.
  4. Primary before fallback — overflow officers (e.g. Bryan) are used only when no primary LO is available in that state.
  5. Round robin among whoever's left in the active tier — rotation is tracked per eligible group so counts stay balanced.
  6. Fallback → Prequal Team if no eligible officer remains.
